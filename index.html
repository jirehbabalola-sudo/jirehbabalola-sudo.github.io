Who I Am

About Me

I am a Substation Engineer & Project Manager at Great River Energy, Maple Grove, MN, holding a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering and currently completing my MBA — both from Bethel University. My career sits at the intersection of deep technical expertise and people-centered leadership.

My journey spans power system protection, relay coordination, SCADA integration, and full-cycle project management. I have led complex substation upgrade projects that measurably improved reliability, efficiency, and regulatory compliance. Beyond the technical, I am a committed advocate for diversity and inclusion — serving as Chair of the People of Color Group at GRE and mentoring the next generation of STEM professionals.

The MBA program at Bethel has sharpened my strategic thinking, financial acumen, and data-driven decision-making. I now approach engineering challenges with a holistic view — balancing operational efficiency, stakeholder needs, global market forces, and ethical leadership grounded in my Christian faith.

"My core purpose is Understanding Hope — creating opportunities, fostering resilience, and believing in greater possibilities for every person I serve."
